STATION FOURTEEN

JESUS IS BURIED

V.

We adore you, O Christ and we praise you.

R.

Because by your Holy Cross you have redeemed the world

READING:

A reading from the Holy Gospel according to JOHN (JOHN 19:40-42)

They took the Body of Jesus, and bound it in linen clothes with the spices, as is the burial customs of the Jews. Now, in the place where he was crucified, there was a garden, and in the garden a new tomb, where no one had ever been laid. So because of the Jewish day of preparation, as the tomb was close at hand, they laid Jesus there.

CHRIST SPEAKS:

So ends my mortal life but now another life begins for Mary and for Magdalene for Peter and John for you my work as man is done. My work within and through the church must now commence. I look to you. You are my friend you are my apostle.

MAN RESPONDS

Lord Jesus, it is hard to be your apostle the demands are great. You have completed your work, what is left is mine, that of my fellowmen that of your church. The teachings you could not impart, let me impart. The sufferings you could not bear, let me bear. The work of love you could not do in you short life on earth. Let me do- all through you for you are all in all.

Ejaculatory Prayer

I love you my Jesus with all my heart above all things, I repent with my whole heart for having offended you, never permit me from separating myself from you again, grant that I may Love you always and then do with me whatever you will.

Hail Mary....

V.

Come to our aid, O Lord!.

R.

Come to our aid.

English Hymn:

While my body here decays; may my soul thy goodness praise Safe in paradise with thee. Amen.

Latin Hymn:

Quando corpus morietur. Fac ut animaae donetur. Paridisi gloria. Amen.
